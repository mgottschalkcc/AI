Educational Guide: Building Your Framework

Conceptual vs. Theoretical

Conceptual Framework: A structure that organizes and guides the research process by identifying key variables, concepts, and relationships.

Theoretical Framework: A set of established theories or perspectives that guide your approach to the research.

Step-by-Step Logic

  1. Identify the Central Phenomenon: What is the "thing" your study is really about?
  2. Identify Major Concepts: If you removed an idea from the framework, would the study fundamentally change? If not, leave it out.
  3. Consider the Paradigm: Your paradigm determines whether you emphasize measurable relationships (Positivist) or meaning-making and lived experience (Constructivist).
